Frequently Asked Questions

How do I choose which boat?

The boat you choose depends on how many people you have coming with you on your holiday.  The largest boats on the Shannon sleep up to twelve and the smallest will sleep up to four.  The larger the boat naturally the bigger the price.  Just be aware that the bed number will include a bed made by converting the kitchen table and also from the seating area of the boat.  Also, tall people need to be aware that some of the beds can be a bit on the short side.  Each boat will have a layout plan showing all the sleeping accommodation on board.  My advice would be to always choose a boat suitable for two people more than your party, it makes for more comfort all around.

How much will the holiday cost?

The cost of the holiday will depend mostly on the boat you choose, the duration of your holiday and the number of hours boating (fuel costs) you wish to do.  Hire companies have boats of all sizes and also of all ages and the bigger and newer the boat the more expensive it will be.  The time of the year makes a big difference too and the most expensive months are July and August, the lease expensive time being March and October. 

What is provided on board?

All boats have at least one bathroom with shower, a galley with a cooker, oven and a small fridge.  Some will have microwave ovens, low voltage appliances and 12v sockets.  Everything needed to live on board is provided including fresh bedding, towels, soap, toilet paper, cutlery and crockery.  Most boats also have a torch and binoculars on board.  Some of the larger boats have bow or stern thrusters.  Some of the newer boats have shore power and new shore power points are being added continuously by Waterways Ireland, a list of these are available on their website.  Link?

Do I need a licence or experience?

No licence or experience is required to hire a boat on the Shannon as full tuition is provided on arrival at the hire base.  Driving a boat takes a little practice as the boats do not react the same way or as quickly as a car.  You will be shown all the tricks of manoeuvring a boat by the staff who are only too happy to help you get it right and they also provide a manual with maps and hints.

Is there a minimum age to hire a cruiser?

Yes the minimum age to hire a cruiser is 21.

Is it possible to hire a boat for less than a week?

It is possible to hire a boat for a period of three or four nights depending on availability.  Most companies facilitate this except in the months of July and August. 

What are the payment terms?

To confirm the booking you will need to pay 33% of the boat hire fee and the balance is paid six weeks before the cruise is due to begin.  For the shorter breaks the full hire fee is required when confirming the booking.  At Shannon Boating we take payment by credit card online or by phone or by any of the payment options listed.  Payment can be made online and our banking details are available on our booking form.

Are the boats insured?

The boats are fully insured against damage with an excess on each boat equal to the refundable damage deposit, which can vary from €850 to €3,200 depending on the boat.  This is paid directly to the hire boat company when you arrive and is fully refundable when the boat is returned undamaged.  More options on boat insurance is listed with each boat such as a damage waiver.

Any hidden costs?

Like with hiring a car, the fuel tanks on the boat will be full when you get the boat.  Depending on how fast you drive the boat and how many hours boating you do you might only have to get the tank filled on your return and the fuel costs calculated.  If you are undertaking a longer cruise there are diesel filling stations at all the main tie up areas.  Most of the boats have a maximum speed of around 10-15Kph which helps with the fuel costs!

Are life jackets and safety equipment provided?

Life jackets are provided for all crew members and will be sized by the hire boat staff.  Fire extinguishers and fire blankets are on board as well as a first aid kit and flare gun for emergencies. 

What do I need to take with me?

You will have to provide your own food and drinks and the hire companies will be happy to organise your groceries delivered to your boat at your request.  Extra towels and rain gear are always a good idea when boating and rubber soled shoes for grip on the deck are helpful. 

Is there heating on the boat?

All of the boats have warm air central heating that runs on its own motor and does not require the engine to be running.  You will need to run the main engine for hot water. 

Do I have to pay to moor the boat?

There is free mooring at every town and village on the length of the Shannon and Erne waterways.  At busy times it is acceptable to moor alongside another boat if there is no free space.  There are some private marinas, like in Athlone, where there is a charge for mooring.

What about locks?

Locks on the River Shannon are operated by lock keepers at a cost of €1.30 per use (since Covid this fee has been waived but could be re-instated at any time).  The opening hours for the locks vary from season to season and will be highlighted in the Captain's book which will be on board.  There are 16 locks on the Shannon Erne Waterway which are all automatic and you will need a swipe card to use these locks.  Swipe cards are available from the hire boat company as well as from many shops along the waterway.  These swipe cards are also used for shower and laundry facilities at various harbours.  Your cruise company will supply information on how to operate the automatic locks and the instructions are also at each lock.  Please allow plenty of time for passage through the locks as a number of boats may be going through together and this takes time.
Portumna Bridge opens on a fixed schedule and the times are also contained in the Captain's book and again vary per season. 

What is the collection and return time for the boat?

The collection time for the boat is 4pm which allows time for the boat to be cleaned and maintained after the previous hire.  It may be possible to get the boats for short breaks out around 2pm unless the marina is very busy.  The boat should be returned and vacated by 10am on the final morning. 

How do I get to the marina?

Most of the companies provide a transfer service from the airports to their marinas as the marinas are mostly in rural areas and difficult to reach by public transport.  Shared transfers can be arranged where possible.  The prices for transfers are available on each company's page.  We would be happy to quote for a private transfer when you are booking your boat or can help you with combining public transport and private transfers if you wish. 

Is there somewhere to park my car?

There is free car parking at all of the marinas in Ireland with the exception of the Emerald Star marinas who charge €6.00 per night.  This parking is not supervised at night but the crime rate is very low in rural Ireland and we have not yet heard of a car being interfered with at any marina on the river.

Can I take a pet on board?

Yes pets can be brought on board at your own risk and will incur an extra cleaning charge of between €60-€80.
