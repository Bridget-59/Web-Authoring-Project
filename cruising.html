Cruiser Choice

Shannon Boating's number one objective is to help you choose the boat that is perfect for you. This will depend on a number of factors such as the number of crew members you have, how much you wish to spend on hiring the boat and how experienced you and your crew are at handling a boat.
Each boat states the maximum number it sleeps and with our knowledge of every model we can rocommend the size that would suit your group. The cost of the boat varies according to the size and age of the boat and costs vary with the different seasons also.
While all boat hire companies give training on how to handle the boat, previous experience will help with handling the bigger boats. Boats with bow and stern thrusters make manouvering a boat much easier.

Cruising Times along the Shannon

One of the most important things to keep in mind when planning your boating holiday on the River Shannon is the time it takes to get from town to town keeping in mind the time spent passing through locks and under bridges.
A cruise on the Shannon is all about relaxation.  The boats go very slowly, you will be overtaken by cyclists and even joggers if they are fast enough, so relax and watch the beautiful scenery go by.  The chart below shows the average cruising times between towns on the River Shannon in hours.  If you are heading upriver to the north read horizontally and if you are heading south read vertically.  Keep in mind also that we can arrange one way hire with hire companies who have two or more hire bases and this will give you more time to explore everything the river and its lakes have to offer or indeed more time to relax and go even slower!
